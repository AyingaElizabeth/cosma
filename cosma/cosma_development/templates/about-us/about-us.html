Who We Are

Building Self-Reliant Communities

COSMA Sustainable Rural Development (COSMA) is a Ugandan non-governmental organization established in 2017, committed to building prosperous and self-reliant rural communities in Uganda.

We deliver integrated agriculture and education programs through a community-driven development approach that strengthens the incomes of vulnerable rural households. By organizing communities into village cooperatives and enterprise groups, we improve agricultural production and expand income-generating opportunities, enabling families to increase their incomes, improve their livelihoods, and sustainably support their children’s education.

Since our establishment, COSMA has reached over 1,750 farmers directly and 7,000+ individuals indirectly, and supported 120+ children to access and stay in school—strengthening livelihoods and expanding educational opportunities across rural communities

Our Impact
  • 1,750+ farmers directly supported
  • 7,000+ individuals impacted
  • 120+ children supported in school

How We Work

How We Deliver Our Mission

We advance our mission through integrated, community-driven interventions that:

  • Increase agricultural productivity through climate-smart agricultural practices.
  • Expand access to finance and strengthen financial literacy among rural households.
  • Strengthen market access and build collective enterprises for smallholder farmers.
  • Equip vulnerable households – particularly those with limited access to land – with practical, income-generating skills.
  • Enable vulnerable children to access and stay in school.

By linking livelihoods to education, we empower households to not only improve their incomes – but to sustain long-term progress for their children and communities. Learn more about Our Work
