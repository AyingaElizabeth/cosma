Overview

From Sponsorship to Self-Reliance

We keep children in school through targeted education support while strengthening household livelihoods — so families can sustainably finance their children’s education over time.

At COSMA, we believe every child deserves not only access to education, but the opportunity to stay, progress, and complete their education journey. In many rural communities in Uganda, children drop out of school not because they lack potential, but because their families lack the financial means to sustain their education.

For years, we supported vulnerable children through a traditional child sponsorship model, covering school fees and essential needs. While this improved access, it did not fully address a critical challenge: when external support ends, many families are unable to continue financing their children’s education.

To address this, we evolved our approach. Today, our Education Program goes beyond sponsorship to help families build the means to sustain education on their own.

Why the Program Matters

Keeping Children in School Means Staying in Life

Education is not simply about getting children into school — it is about enabling them to stay and complete their education. While school enrollment in Uganda is high, retention and completion remain major challenges, particularly among vulnerable rural households.

90%+

Household Poverty

The Root Cause of Dropout

  • 47.6% of rural households fall within the bottom 40% of the national consumption distribution.
  • 41.8% of female-headed households fall within the poorest 40% nationally.
  • 80% of households cite lack of income as the primary reason children drop out of school.

For many children, the challenge is not starting school — it is staying in school. Without addressing household poverty, dropout cycles are likely to continue.

The Solution

The Enterprise-to-Education (E2E) Model

The Enterprise-to-Education Model is COSMA’s integrated livelihoods–education approach, designed to address the root cause of school dropout: household poverty.

Rather than focusing only on the child, we work with the entire household so families can sustainably support their children’s education over time. Our model links income generation, savings, and education support in one coordinated system.

How the Model Works

Four Interconnected Components

Education Protection

Children receive immediate support to stay in school through school fee support, learning materials, mentorship, and regular monitoring.

Community Social Enterprise Development

Caregivers, especially women, form social enterprises with hands-on skills training in activities such as book production, entrepreneurship, and business management.

Savings & Financial Inclusion

Households join savings and loan groups to build savings, access small loans, and strengthen financial planning and resilience.

Household Transition to Self-Reliance

As incomes improve, families gradually contribute more toward school costs. Over time, external support reduces as households move toward independent education financing.

What Makes It Different
  • Addresses the root cause of school dropout, not just the symptoms.
  • Builds self-reliant families, not just supported children.
  • Links livelihoods directly to education outcomes.
  • Reduces long-term dependency on external support.
What It Achieves
  • Sustained school retention and completion.
  • Increased and stable household incomes.
  • Strong savings culture among families.
  • Transition from dependency to education self-reliance.
