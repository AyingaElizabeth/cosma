The Challenge

Transforming Smallholder Farming into Sustainable Livelihoods

We empower smallholder farmers through village cooperative systems that combine climate-smart agriculture, access to finance, and market linkages – enabling households to increase incomes and build resilience.

Smallholder farmers in rural Uganda face persistent challenges such as erratic rainfall patterns, prolonged dry spells, limited access to quality inputs, weak market systems, and restricted access to finance. These barriers keep productivity low and incomes unstable.

COSMA’s Agricultural Program addresses these challenges through a structured, community-driven development approach that strengthens how farmers produce, finance, and sell.

Why This Program Matters

Agriculture is the backbone of rural livelihoods

Agriculture is the backbone of rural livelihoods in Uganda, yet many smallholder farmers remain trapped in low productivity and low-income cycles.

  • 62.3% of households are engaged in agriculture nationally.
  • Over 70% of Ugandans depend on agriculture for their livelihoods.
  • 80.2% of agricultural households primarily farm for subsistence.
  • Over 60% of smallholder farmers experience low crop productivity.
  • Fewer than 30% of farmers have access to formal or affordable agricultural financing.

The biggest challenge is not lack of effort but rather the system within which farmers operate. Without addressing the systemic barriers, smallholder farming remains a subsistence activity rather than a viable pathway out of poverty for many rural households in Uganda.

Our Approach

The Village Cooperative Model

At the core of our program is the Village Cooperative Model — a community-driven development approach that organizes farmers into structured groups (Village Cooperatives, VCs) to improve how they produce, access finance, and engage with markets.

Through these cooperatives, farmers move from working individually to operating as organized economic actors — strengthening their voice and capacity to drive their own development.

Village Cooperatives serve as platforms through which farmers access bundled support, combining production, financing, and market engagement.

How the Model Works

Integrated support across production, finance, and markets

Production Improvement

Farmers are supported to increase yields and improve soil health through practical, climate-smart agricultural practices.

  • • Training in climate-smart crop production and agronomic practices
  • • Access to quality inputs such as drought-resilient and fast-maturing seeds, and fertilizers
  • • Soil health management and advisory services
  • • Demonstration farms and seasonal technical support

The result: higher yields, improved crop quality, and greater resilience to climate variability.

Agricultural Financing & Financial Literacy

Through Village Cooperatives, farmers participate in savings and loan activities — meeting regularly to save money, build capital, access credit, and strengthen their financial skills.

  • • Agricultural input financing on deferred payment
  • • Village-based savings and loan systems
  • • Training in financial management and planning

These interventions lead to improved access to capital, stronger financial discipline, and increased farm investment.

Connecting Farmers to Profitable Markets

Farmers are supported to sell collectively and access better market opportunities.

  • • Post-harvest handling and crop quality assurance
  • • Aggregation of produce through the village cooperatives
  • • Linkages to reliable buyers
  • • Real-time market information and price updates
  • • Collective selling to strengthen bargaining power

This targeted support leads to better prices, increased sales, and more stable incomes.

A Fully Integrated Livelihood System

A coordinated system for real income growth

What sets COSMA apart is the integration of production, financing, and market access into one coordinated system.

Rather than addressing these challenges in isolation, we support farmers to:

  • • Produce more through climate-smart agricultural practices
  • • Invest confidently through accessible agricultural financing and strengthened financial inclusion
  • • Sell better through structured market linkages

By connecting these elements, we ensure that productivity gains translate into real and sustained income growth — moving farmers beyond subsistence to viable livelihoods.
